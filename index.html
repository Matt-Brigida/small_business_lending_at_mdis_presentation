Background and Literature

Minority-Owned Depository Institutions: A Market Overview

In this report we highlight the difficulties minority-owned small businesses face in obtaining funding from financial institutions. Our findings highlight the role of Minority-Owned Depository Institutions (MDIs) in providing funding to these small businesses, and thereby acting as economic development engines in these communities.

Opportunity Zones

Opportunity zones were created as part of the recent Tax Cut and Jobs Act. The Treasury has identified distressed communities throughout the country. Investment in these communities will receive tax incentives.

The Data

Our panel data are obtained from the Call Reports banks are required to file with the FDIC.

  • Our data set has 710,688 bank-quarter observations, and ranges from January 2001 to December 2017 inclusive.
  • Since some of these observations may contain ‘NA’ (say, for example, a bank has no small-business loans), the following regressions will be run on subsets of the total 710,688 for which a panel without missing observations can be built.
  • We have made the data available here.

Fixed-Effects Model: % Change Small Business Loans

This model investigates the determinants of changes in small-business lending in our panel. We estimate a fixed-effect model with bank fixed effects ($\alpha_{i}$). Each independent variable is lagged one year relative to the dependent variable. We use two dependent variables, the percent change in the (1) dollar amount, and (2) number of small business loans. The model has the following specification:

$\% \Delta SB\ Loans$$ = \beta_1 ($$Capital\ Ratio$$) +$

$+ \beta_2 \frac{Amt.\ SB\ Loans}{TA} + \beta_3$$ROA$$ + \beta_4 \frac{NPA}{TA} + $

$+ \beta_5 \frac{Deposits}{TA} + \beta_6$$log(TA)$$ + \beta_7 (post\ crisis) +$

$ +\beta_8 (fin\ crisis) + \beta_9 (de\ novo) + \beta_{10}TE$

$\beta_{11}(ln(TA)*ROA) + \beta_{12}(ln(TA)*NPA) + $

$\alpha_{i}$$ + \epsilon_{it}$

where SB denotes Small Business, NPA denotes Non-Performing Assets, and TA denotes Total Assets. post crisis is an indicator variable for the years 2012--2015 inclusive. fin. crisis is an indicator for the years 2008 through 2011. de novo indicates the bank is less than 5 years old.
